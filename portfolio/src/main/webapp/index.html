About Me:

Hello! My name is Sergio Ortiz. My passions include coding and playing videogames! I also like solving twisty puzzles such as the Rubik's cube, with my average solve time on the 3x3 lying at slightly under 35 seconds! My favorite language is Java, with Python coming in close second!

I was born in February 11, 2001 in the border city of El Paso, Texas. As a child, I was always gifted in Mathematics. One of my earliest memories of school is impressing my first grade teacher by doing division (pretty sure it was division by 2). During my early teenage years, I was in a bit of a slump in regards to my career choice. I knew engineering would probably utilize my strenghts the most, but I was unsure what branch to pursue. Then, as a sophmore in High School, I got the opportunity to take the introductory Computer Science course at my local community college. After the first week of this class, I was 90% sure I had finally found my career interest and thus, began the journey! Four years later, I am a STEP intern at Google!

My Favorite Videogames:

Here are some of my favorite videogames. There's a lot but these are the ones I could think of when writing this!

Easily my favorite videogame of all time is The Legend of Zelda Breath of the Wild. This adventure was so massive and epic, it's honestly hard to forget. I have now played this game twice, sinking in almost 500 hours into it! I could have easily spent that time on a sophisticated personal project.


Another personal favorite is Cuphead. This game was so challenging, but the feeling of personal accomplishment upon passing a level is unmatched. The artsyle of this game is so unique and fun, with every character and animation hand-drawn to represent old cartoons. I cannot wait for the upcoming DLC!


These are some of my favorite platformers! Although Super Mario 3D World is not commonly liked by fans, it is my favorite 3D platformer simply because it was the first brand new game that I bought on release. Tropical Freeze is probably one of the best 2D platformers ever.
